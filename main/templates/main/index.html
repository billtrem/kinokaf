What is Kinokaf?

Kinokaf is a temporary pop-up film café, bar and micro-cinema currently in development for an undisclosed location in Aberystwyth. The project is designed as a six month pilot, during which we aim to test the idea, shape the space with the community, and begin building a business plan for a potential long-term venue.

We work with spaces in a simple and flexible way, using second-hand furniture, warm lighting and a relaxed DIY approach to create an inviting place to spend time. Kinokaf is intended to feel calm during the day, sociable in the evening, and always centred around film and culture.

Although the details of the final site are still being discussed, our goal is to offer Aberystwyth a creative hub where cinema, coffee, reading, music and conversation all live together.

Cinema

Kinokaf will feature a dedicated cinema area with a large screen and digital projection. Films will run throughout our opening hours, from the moment we open until the time we close. Screenings will use Smoovie, our automated cinema system developed in Wales, which lets us create a smooth, high quality viewing experience without heavy technical setup.

The programme will include independent cinema, documentaries, world films, Welsh storytelling, cult picks, shorts and themed seasons. We want the cinema to feel informal, intimate and welcoming, the kind of room where discovering a new film feels special.

Café and Bar

During the day, Kinokaf will operate as a relaxed café serving good coffee, soft drinks and space for reading, meeting and working. As evening arrives, it becomes a small and friendly bar offering a short list of beers, wines and simple food such as toasties and pastries.

The intention is to keep things straightforward and comfortable without the need for a full kitchen. The atmosphere comes from people, music, film and a space that feels easy to be in.

Library

We plan to include a small film themed library made up of DVDs, world cinema, cult classics, documentaries, film books and related titles. Visitors will be able to browse the shelves and borrow items through a simple loan system.

Over time the collection will grow through donations, new acquisitions and curated selections connected to the cinema programme. The aim is to create a gentle, accessible resource for anyone interested in film and the wider arts.

Gigs and Live Events

Kinokaf will also host occasional small scale live events including music, spoken word, open mic nights, discussions and workshops. The idea is to offer a platform for local creativity in an intimate setting where audiences and performers are close to one another.

The Pilot

This project is being developed as a low impact six month pilot. The aim is to activate an unused space, understand the local appetite for a film café, gather real data on attendance and café use, and explore whether a permanent version could be sustainable.

Plans are ongoing and the final details will be shared once the location and timeline are confirmed. If the pilot is successful, we hope to work toward a long term cultural venue shaped around film, hospitality, reading and community.
