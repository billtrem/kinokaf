About Kinokaf

Kinokaf is a pop-up cinema and cultural space created by filmmaker Will Tremlett and artist Nadia Gillard. Together they combine years of community cinema programming, creative practice, and warm hospitality to build a place where film, conversation, coffee, and culture meet.

The name “Kino” is a widely used European term for independent or arthouse cinemas — small, characterful places dedicated to storytelling, ideas, and gathering. “Kaf” nods to café-bar culture: relaxed, social, welcoming, and woven into daily life.

Kinokaf blends these two worlds: an intimate arthouse-style screening experience with the atmosphere of a neighbourhood café-bar. As a travelling pop-up, we bring film, books, music, and community vibes to towns, villages, and unexpected spaces — wherever the story needs to go.

Cinema

We screen independent films, world cinema, documentaries, cult favourites, and new voices from Wales and beyond — all curated with care. Expect pop-up screenings, filmmaker talks, themed nights, and small, warm, atmospheric events.

Café & Bar

Our café-bar offers excellent coffee, homemade cakes, craft beers, natural wines, and simple, comforting food. A place to meet friends, chat after a film, or simply spend time in a space that feels good.

Loan Library

Our growing collection of books spans film, art, radical politics, poetry, fiction, culture, and brilliant oddities. Borrow something, swap something, or settle in with a warm drink and a new story.

Gigs & Live Events

Kinokaf also hosts small gigs, spoken word nights, workshops, open mic evenings, and creative gatherings. A place for artists, musicians, and the local community to share their work up close.
